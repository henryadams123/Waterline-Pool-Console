How the chemicals interact

TA→pHAlkalinity buffers pH. Fix alkalinity first — with it out of range, pH won't hold and you'll chase it every visit.
ACIDMuriatic acid lowers pH and alkalinity together. To drop alkalinity without tanking pH, add acid in one spot, then aerate.
ASHSoda ash raises pH and nudges alkalinity up. If only alkalinity is low, use baking soda — it lifts alkalinity with a much smaller pH bump.
pH→ORPORP is pH-dependent. Low pH inflates the ORP reading, high pH deflates it — so ORP can look fine while free chlorine is actually low.
CYA↔ClMore stabilizer means you must hold more chlorine — and it suppresses ORP. Keep free chlorine near 7.5% of the CYA reading.
pH→ClHigh pH weakens chlorine. Above pH 8.0 chlorine loses roughly half its punch — balance pH before blaming the sanitizer.
07

Handling rules

  • Add acid to water, never water to acid. Dilute muriatic in a bucket; add in the deep end, away from lights and skimmers.
  • Never mix chemicals. Cal-hypo + trichlor dry is a fire/explosion hazard. Keep acid and chlorine additions hours apart with full circulation.
  • Pump running, one dose at a time. Wait ~15–30 min between different chemicals; retest 4–6 hours after dosing before adding more.
  • Never shock with pH or alkalinity out of range, and don't dose blind — test first, every time.
